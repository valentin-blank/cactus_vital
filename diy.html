DIY Geiger Counter

Interested for a long time in radioactivity subject and about all history facts it constitutes, I decided (just after Fukushima's catastrophe) to build my own DIY Geiger Counter late 2011. Pretty nice project based on Arduino with lots of solderings and fragiles components, doing it was also about getting concrete informations about radiations and how they work. I also needed to ship the Geiger Müller Tube on a private and obscure online shop in China so it would work properly, which was kind of fun and scary, but it worked, and of course radiations were in the average!

LoLShield

The LoL Shield is a charlieplexed LED matrix for the Arduino. The LEDs are individually addressable, so you can use it to display anything in a 9×14 grid (126 led of two pins = 252 holes to solder). Scroll text, play games, display images, or anything else you want to do. C++!
